Apostille & Authentication

An apostille certifies U.S. documents for use in Hague Convention countries. Typical items: birth certificates, marriage certificates, FBI checks, and other documents (please see the U.S. Dept of State authentication list for more details).

  1. Obtain certified copy of the document.
  2. Contact your state Secretary of State or the U.S. Department of State for federal documents.
  3. Submit required forms (e.g., DS-4194 for federal auth).
  4. Allow 2–6 weeks or use an expediting service.

Helpful link: U.S. Dept. of State - Authentications

Visa & Residency Pathways

Countries offering digital nomad or remote worker programs often require proof of income, background checks, and apostilled documents. Examples:

  • Portugal D8 Visa
  • Spain Digital Nomad Visa
  • Costa Rica Remote Work Visa
  • Estonia e-Residency

Financial Readiness

Keep income mobile and documented. Common recommendations to prove financial stability include maintaining 3–6 months of statements and proof of yearly income (many visas reference $30k–$40k as a guideline, but amounts vary). Here are examples of resources to help keep your finances mobile and flexible, along with health and travel insurance options:

  • International banking: Wise, Revolut
  • Health & travel insurance: SafetyWing, IMG Global
  • Passive income ideas: sell digital products, rentals, and obtain affiliate income

Secure Storage & Backups

Keep physical originals in a fireproof/waterproof folder and digital copies in encrypted cloud storage. Share emergency access with a trusted contact or attorney.

Suggested tools: Proton Drive, Google Drive (with 2FA), password manager (1Password, Bitwarden).
